The Essence of Durga Puja 2025

Durga Puja at GGLCA is a radiant celebration of Maa Durga’s triumph, blending Bengali traditions with Bangalore’s multicultural spirit. From October 9-13, 2025, Sobha Lakeview Clubhouse in Bellandur will transform into a vibrant pandal, echoing the grandeur of Kolkata’s UNESCO-recognized Durga Puja. Inspired by our 2024 “Prantik” theme, which celebrated India’s tribal art with sustainable materials, Anandotsab 2025 will immerse you in a dazzling display of clay idols, intricate lighting, and soulful rituals.

Kolkata’s Durga Puja, a global cultural phenomenon, inspires our festivities. Our pandal, adorned with bamboo, jute, and eco-friendly paints, mirrors the artistry of Sreebhumi’s crore-budget setups or Suruchi Sangha’s award-winning designs. From Mahalaya’s Chandipath chants to Dashami’s sindoor khela, every ritual is a testament to devotion. Our 50+ member families, from tech professionals to children, craft decorations, creating a community-driven spectacle that rivals Kolkata’s pandal-hopping culture.

The festival kicks off with Mahalaya, invoking Maa Durga’s arrival, followed by five days of festivities from Shashti to Dashami. Expect dhunuchi naach under twinkling lights, Rabindra Sangeet performances, and dance dramas that capture Bengal’s soul. Our pandal will feature sustainable designs, inspired by Kolkata’s Lalabagan Nabankur, which used 8,000 plants in 2024 to promote reforestation. Food stalls will serve authentic Bengali delicacies like khichuri, luchi, and payesh, creating a sensory feast that transports you to Kolkata’s bustling streets.

Sustainability is at the heart of our celebration. Like Kolkata’s Serader Sera Nirmal Puja Puroskar winners, we use biodegradable materials and non-toxic paints, honoring Maa Durga’s connection to nature. Our 2025 theme will blend tribal art with modern creativity, creating a pandal that’s both a visual marvel and an environmental statement. From the rhythmic beats of the dhak to the fragrance of dhuno, every element invites you to immerse yourself in the magic of Durga Puja.

GGLCA’s Durga Puja is a cultural bridge, uniting Bangalore’s diverse communities in a celebration of art and spirituality. Our pandal, crafted by local artisans and inspired by Kumartuli’s workshops, features handcrafted idols that embody Maa Durga’s divine power. The festival’s economic impact, like Kolkata’s ₹32,000 crore contribution in 2019, highlights its significance as a cultural and economic powerhouse. Join us to experience pandal-hopping, live performances, and the warmth of community, creating memories that resonate with Bengal’s heart.

From the Kola Bou ritual to the visarjan procession, every moment is steeped in tradition. Our cultural programs feature local talents, from children performing folk dances to professionals staging Tagore’s plays. The pandal’s lighting, inspired by Kolkata’s dazzling displays, creates a magical ambiance, while food stalls offer a taste of Bengal’s culinary heritage. Anandotsab 2025 is a celebration of life, creativity, and unity, inviting all to join in the joy of Maa Durga’s blessings.

Our Achievements

Since 2017, GGLCA has been a cultural beacon in Green Glen Layout, Bellandur, fostering unity through vibrant festivals and community initiatives. Our achievements reflect our commitment to preserving Bengali heritage while embracing Bangalore’s multicultural ethos, inspired by Kolkata’s world-renowned Durga Puja.

  • Innovative Themes: Recognized for our 2024 “Prantik” theme, celebrating India’s tribal art with sustainable decorations, crafted by 50+ member families.
  • Annual Events: Hosting 8+ cultural events yearly, including Confluence, Kali Puja, and Saraswati Puja, uniting diverse communities.
  • Literary Contributions: Publishing the Annual Souvenir – Anandotsab and e-magazine Udbhas, showcasing community talents.
  • Community Welfare: Organizing free health camps and exclusive member discounts, enhancing local well-being.
  • Cultural Hub: Building a platform for professionals and families to celebrate Indian traditions, earning recognition in Bangalore.
  • Eco-Friendly Practices: Adopting sustainable materials, inspired by Kolkata’s Serader Sera Nirmal Puja Puroskar winners, for pandal decorations.

While specific awards are not documented, GGLCA’s excellence mirrors Kolkata’s award-winning pujas, recognized by Asian Paints Sharad Shamman since 1985. Our 2024 pandal, with its tribal art focus, drew thousands, much like Kolkata’s 3,000+ Barowari pujas in 2022. By collaborating with local artisans and leveraging community talent, we create pandals that rival the artistry of Chetla Agrani or Kumartuli Park.

Our commitment to sustainability, inspired by Kolkata’s green pujas, ensures our celebrations honor Maa Durga’s connection to nature. From biodegradable decorations to community-driven events, GGLCA fosters a legacy of creativity and unity, making Anandotsab a highlight in Bangalore’s cultural calendar.
